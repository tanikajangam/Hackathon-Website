A hackathon is typically a 24-72 hour programming event (in this case, 48), where participants work together to innovate, create, and pitch their projects that fall under one of the themes (see tracks) of the event. You may choose any technologies, programming languages, platforms, or services to build your project. There will be workshops, challenges, mini-events, and opportunities to network with sponsors and mentors throughout the event.


You can sign up for the hackathon here), and you'll be added to our mailing list and start receiving updates and more information as the event approaches. You can leave and rejoin at any time throughout the event (although we don't recommend missing out on all the awesome workshops and events).


Since this is a virtual event, anyone from around the world can participate. There is no age or experience requirement, and we welcome people from all backgrounds.

No coding experience is required to attend the event. If you want to compete and submit your project to win a prize, it is recommended that you have experience with programming and developing projects. But, even if you don't want to engage in the competitive aspect, KodaHacks is a great opportunity to learn new technologies and languages. For beginners, there will be workshops, speakers, and mentors, all there to help you fast-track your development skills.

That's no problem at all! Whether you want to go all out and stay up hacking away on your project or take this one easy and focus on learning and networking, the choice is yours! Organizers and mentors are present throughout the event and in the days and weeks leading up to it to help if you get stuck. We’ll also send out a hacker guide through email a few weeks before the event so that you know what to expect.

All you need is a working computer and an internet connection (and probably a lot of caffiene and broccoli!).


Though you can work alone, we strongly recommend working in a group to get the most out of the hackathon experience. Team sizes can vary between 1-4 people.


Definitely! Our judges will select the best project submissions, and the winning teams will receive cash prizes of up to $2,000 and tons of free services and technologies. Specific prizes will be announced as the date of the event approaches.

Thanks to our sponsors, this event is absolutely free to attend! There is no cost associated with participating, attending workshops, or submitting projects.

You can start brainstorming ideas for your project, but you cannot write any code before the hacker clock starts. Any projects started before the event or projects submitted to other hackathons will be disqualified.
